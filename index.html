Validity

Although as early as 2004 Joan McComas points out in his research that VR education can be effective in some scenarios. The application of VR in teaching has always been controversial. One of the reasons may be that modern education has evolved over a long period of time, and the newly born VR technology is not mature in the research of different types of teaching(StéphaniePhilippe,2020). This has led to a lack of relevant theoretical basis and research on the promotion of courses based on VR as a basic tool, and more schools have adopted traditional teaching methods, because at least they are proven effective.

Reference:

McComas, J., MacKay, M., & Pivik, J. (2002). Effectiveness of Virtual Reality for Teaching Pedestrian Safety. CyberPsychology & Behavior, 5(3), 185–190. https://doi.org/10.1089/109493102760147150

Philippe, S., Souchet, A. D., Lameras, P., Petridis, P., Caporal, J., Coldeboeuf, G., & Duzan, H. (2020). Multimodal teaching, learning and training in virtual reality: a review and case study. Virtual Reality & Intelligent Hardware, 2(5), 421–442. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.vrih.2020.07.008

Limited applied disciplines and high costs

It must be noted that not all disciplines have comparative use of VR as a teaching tool. For example, in basic mathematics and language subjects, paper-based classrooms are effective enough, and it is wasteful to introduce VR as a teaching tool. At the same time, while VR equipment imposes higher equipment requirements on schools, it also increases learning consumption in disguise, which may lead to an overall increase in tuition fees, which further threatens the fairness of education while increasing the cost of learning.

VR school paradox

Fransson point out in her article "The challenges of using head mounted virtual reality in K-12 schools from a teacher perspective" that the smaller the school, the easier it is for emerging schools to transition to VR teaching, while those with well-established teaching systems are more likely to transition to VR. On the contrary, it is difficult for large schools to get close to VR technology. At the same time, the quality of teaching in small schools is very unreliable. Even from the data point of view, in the 10 years from 2010 to 2020, a large number of small VR teaching institutions appeared and disappeared, while those really excellent schools were very Difficult to complete the transformation

Reference:

Fransson, G., Holmberg, J., & Westelius, C. (2020). The challenges of using head mounted virtual reality in K-12 schools from a teacher perspective. Education and Information Technologies, 25(4), 3383–3404. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10639-020-10119-1
