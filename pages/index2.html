The over optimistic estimate

Many studies like J Adv Med Educ Prof (2018) and Polina Häfner (2013) point out that the application of VR teaching tools has greatly improved the effect of medical and engineering equipment operation training. At the same time, compared to using real equipment to operate, VR tools also greatly reduce the risk and equipment loss, which makes the application prospects of VR in training look very optimistic. In fact, this is also the most extensive application scenario of current VR technology. However, the development of VR technology for different training scenarios is very slow. This may be because the professionals who apply these tools need extremely high professionalism, and the developers of VR technology need to learn for a long time to develop the corresponding VR environment, and these However, the replacement speed of the field is extremely fast, and the technology that has just been developed is often faced with the problem of being eliminated.

The high costs

The problem of high cost and development appears in almost every field of VR. Unlike traditional products, the development of VR products has the characteristics of long cycle, high requirements and uncertain returns. This has discouraged many emerging companies, and the VR systems developed by established companies are too expensive.

Overlap between real and reality

Falah(2014) points out that although the training in the VR environment is very effective, the cost of trial and error for students in the VR environment is too low, which makes it possible for imperfect technicians to make irreversible mistakes in the face of completely different situations in reality. Mistakes, which are especially deadly in anatomical and clinical medicine where extreme precision is required, and the blurring of the boundaries between virtual reality and reality have a negative impact on this issue.

Reference:

Falah, J., Khan, S., Alfalah, T., Alfalah, S. F. M., Chan, W., Harrison, D. K., & Charissis, V. (2014, August 1). Virtual Reality medical training system for anatomy education. IEEE Xplore. https://doi.org/10.1109/SAI.2014.6918271
